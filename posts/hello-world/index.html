Hello, World?

August 7, 2022 · 3 min · 471 words · Me | Suggest Changes

Not an intriguing topic to write as my first blog post. Bear with me. I just happen to start murmuring something emerging in my head. Is it the main reason most bloggers do? I’ll have to image so. So don’t tell me your answer.

I don’t remember in which language I wrote my first “hello world” program. My best guess would be Pascal or C, as I don’t recall doing so using Basic on my first Apple II computer.

These days, however, I write hello-world programs regularly, in an embarrassingly high frequency. I do know the main reason: “why the hack there are so many new tech-kids on the block?” I’m sure it is the main reason for most people (modulo get-started-guide authors). I’ll have to image so. So don’t tell me your answer either.

As a non-native English speaker, what I really want to murmur today is this minor inquiry: “What is the actual text you print in your hello world program(s)?” Well, my absent-minded usual choices are:

  • hello world
  • hello, world
  • Hello, World
  • Hello World
  • Hello, world.

Ignoring the grammatical hiccups, only recently had I become aware of the “more authenticated” versions:

  • hello, world!
  • Hello, world!

I know it is not a big deal (or is it?). If I had a smarter spelling checker, maybe some annoying red wavy underlines would have notified me earlier. But my inner voice emerges anyway: “Why the exclamation!”

  • Does it want to come out and greet the world so badly?
  • Does it think “what takes you so long” and just want to tease me?
  • Does it sense any loneliness in me and just try to lighten the atmosphere?

If it is for the last, maybe I should change the greeting to “Hello, ${USER} <3”, or something more aggressive? I guess it’s unwise to over do it in such a cloud-native, open-source world nowadays, whether or not you make sure to commit stuff only to private repos.

Maybe I just think too much and miss the obvious: “it just means excitement!” I agree. After minutes/hours of cloning, following, copy and pasting, what is the proper way to express the joy when “it” makes its first toddler move. Can’t video-tape it, can’t take a selfie with it, can’t tweet it, can’t blog it (except get-started-guide authors). What could be the better way than imagining that the newly born creature must have shared the same feeling with us. We all know it’s still a long way to go, but for now, just place an exclamation mark, take a rest, and see if we can conquer the obstacles lying ahead later, er… or not.

What was in the “C Programming Language” author’s mind? Well, we all can google it and get some approximation. I do enjoy the yearn for creating something lively and friendly, doing no harm.
